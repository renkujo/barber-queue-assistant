# Product Brief — Barber Queue Assistant

## Working title

Barber Queue Assistant

## Product idea

A lightweight web app that helps a one-owner barber shop manage daily queues, bookings, walk-ins, customer status, and notifications.

The owner is also the barber, so the product must reduce interruptions instead of creating admin work.

## Core problem

A one-person barber shop has limited attention and capacity. The owner cuts hair, answers messages, remembers bookings, handles walk-ins, updates customers, and decides who is next.

Common pain points:

- customers repeatedly ask whether the shop is open, available, or busy;
- customers ask how long they need to wait;
- bookings, walk-ins, phone calls, and LINE/Facebook messages are easy to forget or overlap;
- the owner may be cutting hair and cannot respond quickly;
- no-shows waste valuable appointment slots;
- customers may arrive without knowing queue status.

## Product principle

This is not only a booking system. It is a daily queue assistant for a single barber.

The system should:

- answer common customer questions automatically;
- keep every queue source in one daily list;
- make owner actions fast, large, and obvious;
- prevent schedule collisions;
- notify customers when useful;
- let the owner override the system at any time.

## Target users

### Primary user

The shop owner/barber.

Characteristics:

- works alone;
- has little time to operate a dashboard;
- may use a phone while standing or between haircuts;
- needs today-first information, not complex salon analytics.

### Secondary users

Customers who want to:

- check whether the shop is available;
- book a time;
- get a walk-in queue;
- track their queue status;
- receive updates when their queue is near.

## Channel strategy

The app should not require every customer to enter through LINE LIFF.

Supported entry points:

- normal public web link;
- QR code at shop;
- LINE OA rich menu;
- Facebook/Instagram bio link;
- direct link sent by owner.

LINE OA is useful as a channel for entry and notifications, but the core product should still work for customers without LINE identity.

## MVP positioning

Help the owner know:

- who is being served now;
- who is next;
- who has booked today;
- who is waiting;
- who is late or no-show risk;
- whether the shop should keep accepting queues today.
